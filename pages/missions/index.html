Week 3 — Active

“The final truth lives in the lesson. Unpack the standard. Build the plan. The AI will show you what the eye cannot see.”

Deliverable 01 — Unpack an ELA Standard  ·  Up to 100 pts

  • 1
    Choose your grade-level ELA standard. Select one official ELA standard from the grade you teach. You may choose any standard, use a district Priority Standard, or ask ChatGPT: “Suggest ELA standards for Grade ___ that work well with a cultural dance-themed lesson.” Copy the full standard code and text into your working document.
    Example: RI.3.7 — Use information gained from illustrations and words in a text to demonstrate understanding.
  • 2
    Ask AI to unpack the standard. Paste your standard into ChatGPT with this prompt: “Unpack this ELA standard into clear, teachable sub-skills, including key verbs, student actions, vocabulary, and mastery expectations.” Save the unpacked version.
  • 3
    Select ONE sub-skill to anchor your lesson. Choose the sub-skill that best fits an interdisciplinary cultural activity. Strong options: interpreting visuals, describing movement or sequences, identifying key ideas, explaining relationships, comparing cultural practices.
  • 4
    Submit Deliverable 01. Your submission must include: the exact ELA standard with code, your ChatGPT prompts, the unpacked standard, and the sub-skill you selected.

Deliverable 02 — Build Your ELA, Social Studies, or Arts Lesson Plan  ·  Up to 100 pts

Important: Cultural dance is the context for your ELA lesson — not a performance requirement. Students learn through the context of cultural dance. No one needs to physically perform anything.

  • 1
    Choose your cultural dance tradition as lesson context. Select one culturally grounded dance tradition to anchor your lesson:
    Korean options: Fan Dance (부채춤), Talchum (mask dance), Samgomu Drum Dance
    Latin options: Ballet Folklórico, Cumbia, Salsa
    Students will analyze, read about, and discuss this dance — not perform it.
  • 2
    Select at least three movement phrases in Korean or Spanish. These phrases should appear naturally within reading, writing, speaking, or visual analysis activities in your lesson.
    Examples: “Da un paso lateral” (side step) · “부채 펼치기” (fan opening) · “Sigue el ritmo” (follow the rhythm)
  • 3
    Ask AI for three lesson ideas, then build the full plan. Tell ChatGPT: “Give me three lesson ideas that teach [your sub-skill] using [your chosen dance] and these movement phrases: [list].” Choose one idea, then ask: “Turn this into a full lesson plan using the 5E model / Direct Instruction / PBL.”
  • 4
    Finalize and submit. Your lesson plan must include: your ELA sub-skill, the cultural dance context, at least three Korean or Spanish movement phrases, learning activities, vocabulary, and an exit task or demonstration of understanding.

“The best investigators don’t just solve cases — they teach others how to think. Design a standards-based ELA, Social Studies, or Arts lesson that applies the standard to the context of cultural dance. Students write about themes, compare and contrast costumes and steps, or analyze movement as text — no performance required.”

ELA Standard Unpacking Guide

Your lesson plan must be built around a real ELA standard. Use the California 3rd Grade ELA Standard RI.3.7 as your model example:

"Use information gained from illustrations (e.g., maps, photographs) and the words in a text to demonstrate understanding of the text (e.g., where, when, why, and how key events occur)."

To unpack this standard for your lesson:

"ChatGPT, help me unpack California ELA Standard RI.3.7 for a K-Pop themed lesson. Identify the key sub-skills, suggest an AI tool students can use to practice the standard, and write a lesson objective that connects to the Top K-Pop investigation narrative."

You may use any K-8 ELA standard from your grade level. RI.3.7 is provided as a starting example.
