Deliverable 01

Unpack a Grade-Level ELA Standard Using AI

Worth up to 100 Points

  • 1
    Choose Your ELA Standard (Your Grade Level Only). Select one official ELA standard from the grade you teach — reference the exact standard code and full text in your submission. You may choose any ELA standard from your grade level, select a district Priority Standard, use a Guaranteed & Viable Standard, or ask ChatGPT: "Suggest ELA standards for Grade ___ that would work well with a cultural dance–themed lesson."

    Example — California 3rd Grade ELA Standard RI.3.7: "Use information gained from illustrations (e.g., maps, photographs) and the words in a text to demonstrate understanding of the text." This standard works beautifully with a multimedia dance lesson because it requires students to interpret visuals and connect them to text — just like analyzing cultural dance images or performance clips. Copy the full standard (with the code) into your working document.
  • 2
    Ask AI to Unpack the Standard. Paste your chosen standard into ChatGPT. Prompt example: "Unpack this ELA standard into clear, teachable sub-skills, including key verbs, student actions, vocabulary, and mastery expectations." AI will break the standard into smaller, teachable parts. Save this unpacking.
  • 3
    Select ONE Sub-Skill to Anchor Your Lesson. Choose the sub-skill that best fits an interdisciplinary dance activity. Strong examples: interpreting visuals (perfect for cultural dance images or video clips), describing movement or sequences, identifying key ideas or details, explaining relationships, comparing cultural practices. Pick ONE sub-skill to use for Deliverable 2.
  • 4
    Ask AI to Clarify or Refine Your Sub-Skill. If needed, ask: "Explain this sub-skill for Grade ___ with examples." / "Show how this sub-skill connects to cultural dance or movement." / "Rewrite this sub-skill in student-friendly language." Save any refinements you generate.
  • 5
    Finalize Deliverable 1. Your submission must include: the exact ELA standard (with the standard number), your prompt(s) to ChatGPT, the unpacked standard, and the sub-skill you selected. This becomes the foundation of the lesson you will build in Deliverable 2.

Deliverable 02

Create an Interdisciplinary Lesson

Worth up to 100 Points

  • 1
    Review Your Selected Sub-Skill. Look back at the one sub-skill you chose in Deliverable 1. This will be the literacy focus of your lesson. Everything you design must help students practice and demonstrate this sub-skill.
  • 2
    Choose Your Cultural Dance Connection. Select one culturally grounded dance tradition to anchor your lesson:
    • Korean options: Fan Dance, Talchum, Samgomu Drum Dance
    • Latin options: Ballet Folklórico, Cumbia, Salsa
    This dance serves as the content context for your ELA lesson — NOT as a dance class. Students do not need to perform the dance; they learn through the context of cultural dance.
  • 3
    Select at Least Three Movement Phrases. Choose three dance-related words or phrases in Korean or Spanish that will appear naturally within the lesson. Examples: Da un paso lateral (side step) · Gira a la derecha (turn to the right) · Sigue el ritmo (follow the rhythm) · 부채 펼치기 (fan opening movement) · 리듬에 맞춰 (to the rhythm). These phrases should be meaningfully used during reading, writing, speaking, or analyzing dance visuals.
  • 4
    Ask AI for Three Lesson Ideas. Tell ChatGPT: "Give me three lesson ideas that teach this ELA sub-skill using my chosen cultural dance and these Korean/Spanish movement phrases." Choose one of the ideas or mix elements to create your own.
  • 5
    Build Your Final Lesson Plan Using AI. Paste your chosen idea into ChatGPT and request a full lesson, e.g.: "Turn this idea into a full lesson plan using the 5E model / Direct Instruction / PBL / Cooperative Learning." Your final plan must include: your ELA sub-skill, the cultural dance context, at least three Korean or Spanish movement phrases, learning activities, vocabulary, and an exit task or demonstration of understanding. Refine as needed: pacing, clarity, supports, extensions, differentiation.
  • 6
    Save and Prepare Your Lesson for Submission. Your final Deliverable 2 submission must include: your final AI-supported lesson plan, your selected cultural dance, your three Korean or Spanish movement phrases, the lesson format/model you used, and any prompts you used with AI.

Your Mission

In Trove 03, teams use AI video tools to create a choreographed dance, then design an interdisciplinary lesson plan for students that uses dance to explore ELA, Social Studies, or World Language / International Language standards. Students write about themes, compare and contrast costumes and steps, or analyze movement as text — no physical performance required.

Standards Unpacking Guide — ELA · Social Studies · World Language

Your lesson plan must be built around a real ELA, Social Studies, or World Language / International Language standard. Use the California 3rd Grade ELA Standard RI.3.7 as your model example:

"Use information gained from illustrations (e.g., maps, photographs) and the words in a text to demonstrate understanding of the text (e.g., where, when, why, and how key events occur)."

To unpack this standard for your lesson:

"ChatGPT, help me unpack California ELA Standard RI.3.7 for a K-Pop themed lesson. Identify the key sub-skills, suggest an AI tool students can use to practice the standard, and write a lesson objective that connects to the Top K-Pop investigation narrative."

You may use any K-8 ELA, Social Studies, or World Language / International Language standard from your grade level. RI.3.7 is provided as a starting example.

Step by Step

  • 1
    Unpack a standard. Choose a K-8 ELA, Social Studies, or World Language / International Language standard from your grade level. Use the Oracle of the Lesson Design Rubric GPT to unpack the standard — identify the key sub-skills, a lesson objective, and how K-Pop culture connects to the concept.
  • 2
    Select movement phrases. Choose at least three movement phrases in Korean or Spanish. These phrases should appear naturally within reading, writing, speaking, or visual analysis activities in your lesson.
  • 3
    Ask AI for three lesson ideas, then build the full plan. Tell ChatGPT: "Give me three lesson ideas that teach [your sub-skill] using [your chosen dance] and these movement phrases: [list]." Choose one idea, then ask: "Turn this into a full lesson plan using the 5E model / Direct Instruction / PBL."
  • 4
    Finalize and submit. Your lesson plan must include: your chosen standard sub-skill, the cultural dance context, at least three Korean or Spanish movement phrases, learning activities, vocabulary, and an exit task or demonstration of understanding.
