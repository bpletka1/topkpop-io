The Case.

71 million subscribers. One saboteur. The debut that almost never happened.

The Incident

The Debut Is In Danger.

CEO Minseo — Top K-Pop Entertainment

Top K-Pop Entertainment stands at the pinnacle of the global Hallyu wave. With 71 million subscribers and a debut concert that would cement the company's legacy, CEO Minseo had built an empire from nothing — a vision of Korean pop culture that had captured the hearts of fans on every continent. The company's next supergroup was days away from their announcement. The world was watching.

But on the eve of the most anticipated debut in the company's history, something went wrong. Critically wrong. The lights failed mid-rehearsal. Choreography files were corrupted beyond recovery. The debut track disappeared from the production server. Costumes were destroyed. Catering was poisoned. Rumors spread through the building like smoke. Someone inside Top K-Pop Entertainment had sabotaged everything — deliberately, methodically, and with intimate knowledge of the operation.

"This was not an accident. Someone in this building wanted this debut to fail. I need Detective Recruits who can use AI to find the truth before the lights go down."

— CEO Minseo, Emergency Briefing

CEO Minseo immediately contacted H.A.L.O. — the Hallyu Artificial Intelligence Liaison Organization — and requested their top investigator, Anna Im. Anna assembled a team of AI-powered Detective Recruits: educators trained in artificial intelligence tools who would analyze the evidence, interrogate the suspects, and uncover the saboteur before the debut date.

Six suspects. Three Treasure Troves of clues. Four weeks to crack the case. The saboteur is one of the six people closest to the debut — each with a motive, each with an alibi, and each hiding something the AI tools will help you uncover.

That team is you.

The Investigation

The Evidence Board.

Every Detective Recruit begins with the same evidence board. Six suspects. Three Treasure Troves of clues. Four weeks to crack the case. The saboteur is one of the six people closest to the debut — each with a motive, each with an alibi, and each hiding something the AI tools will help you uncover.

Week 1 — Treasure Trove 01

Create your AI-generated K-Pop idol avatar and promotional poster. Establish your trainee identity with a name, idol skills, and group tagline in Korean and Spanish. Your first clues are hidden in the mission details.

Week 2 — Treasure Trove 02

Produce the Evidence Song. Write multilingual lyrics in English, Korean, and Spanish that weave in the clues you have collected. Generate an AI music track and create a music video. The song itself is a piece of evidence.

Week 3 — Treasure Trove 03

Teach the Case. Design an AI-integrated ELA lesson plan that unpacks an ELA standard, connects K-Pop culture to classroom learning, and incorporates a team cultural dance performance. The final clues are embedded here.

Days 29–31 — Collaboration & Final Accusation

Teams collaborate, compare notes, and submit their Final Accusation. Name the saboteur. Back it up with evidence from all three Treasure Troves. Only one team can be right — and points alone will not win the game.

Day 32 — The Final Reveal

The saboteur is unmasked. The leaderboard is finalized. The team with the highest score AND the correct accusation wins the investigation and saves the debut.

The Client

Meet CEO Minseo.

CEO Minseo

Founder & Chief Executive Officer — Top K-Pop Entertainment

Minseo is a legendary figure in the K-Pop industry, revered not just for his musical talents but for his profound impact on the genre's evolution. Born in the vibrant city of Seoul, Minseo was captivated by the burgeoning K-Pop scene from a young age. His journey began as a trainee in one of Korea's most prestigious entertainment agencies, where his unparalleled dedication and natural flair for music and dance quickly set him apart.

Rising to fame in the late 1990s with a groundbreaking boy group, Minseo became an emblem of the Hallyu wave, propelling Korean pop culture onto the global stage. His group's innovative blend of catchy melodies, intricate choreographies, and visually stunning performances captivated audiences worldwide, setting new standards for K-Pop artists. After a decade of unprecedented success, Minseo embarked on a solo career — exploring new musical territories and continually reinventing his image. His solo albums, a fusion of various genres, received critical acclaim and solidified his status as a K-Pop icon.

Beyond his musical achievements, Minseo is known for his philanthropic efforts and advocacy for artists' rights, using his influence to foster a more inclusive and supportive industry. As a mentor to aspiring singers and a figurehead in numerous cultural initiatives, he has played a pivotal role in nurturing the next generation of K-Pop talent.

Today, Minseo's legacy is not only his vast discography and accolades but also his contribution to shaping K-Pop as a global phenomenon. Now, on the eve of his company's most important debut, someone has betrayed him. Minseo is not a man who asks for help easily — but he knows that finding the truth requires tools he does not have. That is why he called H.A.L.O. That is why he called you.
