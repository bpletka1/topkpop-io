Frequently Asked Questions

Got Questions?

Do I need prior AI experience to play?

No prior AI experience is required. The game is designed to teach AI tools through the process of completing the missions. Each Treasure Trove introduces new tools progressively — you learn by doing, not by studying.

Do I need to sing or dance?

No. While Treasure Trove 03 includes a team cultural dance performance, this is a celebration of cultural learning — not a performance competition. The dance is evaluated on cultural connection and participation, not technical skill. The music in Treasure Trove 02 is AI-generated, not performed.

How are teams formed?

Teams of 2-4 educators register together. You can form teams with colleagues from the same school or across different schools in the same district. Each team designates a team captain who manages submissions and the final accusation.

How is scoring handled?

In Phase 2, scoring will be fully automated — your artifacts are evaluated by the AI rubric engine the moment you submit, and your score appears on the leaderboard within seconds. During Phase 1, scoring is handled manually by the game administrator.

What happens if two teams tie on points?

The tie-breaker is the Final Accusation. If two teams have equal points and both name the correct saboteur, the team that submitted their Treasure Trove 01 earliest wins. If neither team names the correct saboteur, neither team wins.

When does the game start?

The start date is set by your school or district administrator. Games can begin on any Monday with as little as two weeks of advance registration. There is no fixed annual calendar — each game is run on-demand for your district.

Can the game be run for an entire school district?

Yes — the game is designed to scale to district-wide professional development. Teams from multiple schools compete on the same leaderboard. Contact us through the registration form to discuss district licensing and facilitation options.

Official Rules

The Rules of Engagement.

Rule 01

Register Before Day 1

All team members must be registered before the game start date. Late registrations are not accepted once the first Treasure Trove has unlocked.

Rule 02

Artifacts Must Be AI-Generated

All submitted artifacts must be created using AI tools. Hand-drawn or manually produced artifacts are not eligible for scoring.

Rule 03

Korean and Spanish Are Required

Each artifact must include the required Korean and Spanish language elements as specified in the Treasure Trove briefing. Missing language elements result in point deductions.

Rule 04

The Accusation Is Final

Once the Final Accusation is submitted, it cannot be changed. Teams have the full collaboration period (Days 29-31) to review their evidence. Choose wisely.

Rule 05

Points Alone Do Not Win

The winning team must have both the highest score AND the correct accusation. A perfect-scoring team that names the wrong saboteur does not win.

Rule 06

Admin Decisions Are Final

The game administrator reserves the right to award manual bonus points, disqualify submissions that violate the rules, and make final determinations on tie-breaker scenarios.
